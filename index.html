IS 101: Advanced Presentation and Reasoning

Master the art of effective communication, critical thinking, and persuasive presentation in the digital age

Learning Objectives

Professional Presentation Skills

Develop advanced techniques for creating compelling presentations using modern tools and design principles that engage and inform diverse audiences.

Critical Reasoning

Master logical thinking, argument analysis, and evidence evaluation to construct well-reasoned arguments and identify fallacies in reasoning.

Public Speaking Excellence

Build confidence and skills in oral communication, including voice modulation, body language, and audience interaction techniques.

Data Visualization

Learn to transform complex information into clear, impactful visual representations that support your reasoning and enhance understanding.

Course Components

Theoretical Foundations

Explore communication theory, rhetoric principles, cognitive psychology, and the science behind effective persuasion and reasoning.

Practical Applications

Hands-on experience with presentation software, multimedia tools, and emerging technologies for creating dynamic presentations.

Case Study Analysis

Examine real-world presentations and arguments from business, academia, and public discourse to identify effective strategies.

Performance Assessment

Regular presentation opportunities with peer feedback, self-reflection, and instructor evaluation to track progress and improvement.
